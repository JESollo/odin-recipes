Best Pasta Recipe: make pasta for athletes that want to build lean mass for better sports performance!

While hard work and sweat are made in the gym, athletes are truly made in the kitchen!

The perfect athlete is like a well-tuned sports car; it will perform based on the fuel you give it. So unless you want to run your car into the ground, you better give it premium fuel to maximize performance!

Now, for all you new to preparing meals for athletes (whether you're aiming to fuel a significant other, child or turn yourself into a future athlete) it is important to include the premium fuel required for consumption: carbohydrates, proteins and fats.

Without getting into the nitty-gritty of sports-science and nutrition, the bottom-line is: if you're going to be working hard, you're going to need to consume calories! And not just any calories! Athletes will need to consume PLENTY of carbohydrates, proteins and fats to not only supplement the loss of fuel from physical activities (running, game-practice, strength training etc.) BUT ALSO to repair muscle fibre while packing on more muscle mass. That's A LOT to keep in mind while cooking!

While this may seem like a barrier-of-entry for living a healthy lifestyle, it really doesn't have to be that complicated!

That's the beauty of pasta! Pasta is so versatile and nutrient dense - and super easy to prepare - that it checks all the boxes required for maintaining an athlete!

For example, take a look at the nutritional content of this pasta recipe!

Let's Begin!

Ingredients:

  • 1 tbsp. olive oil
  • 200g mushrooms (sliced)
  • 200g mushrooms (sliced)
  • 500g chicken breast (diced)
  • 1 tbsp. Cajun seasoning
  • Salt to taste
  • 500g pasta
  • 500ml chicken stock
  • 100g light cream cheese
  • Chopped chives to garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large non-stick pan.
    1. Add the red onion, mushrooms, and pepper strips and cook for a few minutes until softened.
    2. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  2. Add a little more olive oil to the pan if necessary and then add your chicken pieces.
    1. Fry until completely cooked through and no longer pink, then add the veg back in along with the Cajun seasoning and salt to taste.
    2. Stir well to combine.
  3. Next add the pasta and chicken stock, cover the pan, and leave to simmer for 15 minutes.
  4. Stir through the cream cheese to make a thick, creamy sauce.
    1. Serve immediately with chopped chives to garnish or portion up for your lunches later in the week.
